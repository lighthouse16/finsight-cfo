# Financial Model Governance & Guardrails

This document outlines the scope, methodologies, limitations, and governance guidelines for the deterministic finance and advisory models implemented in the Softform Financial Intelligence UI and backend.

## 1. Deterministic vs. Indicative Calculations

All financial models in this repository are **deterministic** and **rules-based**. They consume raw financial inputs (ratios, cash flows, balance sheet statements, receivables ageing ledgers) and apply fixed, explainable financial formulas to produce planning metrics.

- **Deterministic:** The outputs are calculated from mathematical formulas (such as the logistic proxy for probability of default, or the discounted cash flow model for business valuation). Given the same inputs, the models will always produce the exact same outputs.
- **Indicative:** All outputs are labeled as **indicative planning support** or **candidate structures**. They are designed to support relationship managers and CFOs in exploring scenarios. They do **not** represent a formal credit decision or loan commitment.

## 2. Model Calibration & Limitations

### 2.1 uncalibrated_proxy Status
The Probability of Default (PD) estimate utilizes a standard logistic proxy formula:
$$ Z = \beta_0 + \beta_1(\text{DSCR}) + \beta_2(\text{Debt Ratio}) + \beta_3(\text{Margin}) + \beta_4(\text{Collateral Adjustment}) $$
$$ \text{PD} = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-Z}} $$

- **Not Calibrated:** This proxy is **not** calibrated to historical credit default databases. The weights and baseline intercept are assumptions-based demonstration values only.
- **Data Dependency:** The uncalibrated proxy is highly sensitive to input completeness. If key inputs like DSCR or operating margins are missing, default placeholders are used, reducing the confidence score.

### 2.2 Data Needed for Future Bank-Grade Calibration
To transition from an uncalibrated planning proxy to a formal, bank-grade regulatory PD model, the following datasets and processes are required:
1. **Historical Default Data:** At least 5–7 years of historical default and credit loss data matching the specific SME segment in Hong Kong.
2. **Model Validation:** Statistical calibration using Logistic Regression, Survival Analysis, or Machine Learning (e.g. XGBoost) with formal Out-of-Time (OOT) and Cross-Validation testing.
3. **Statistical Significance Testing:** P-value, Gini coefficient, and ROC/AUC validation of each financial coefficient.
4. **Macroeconomic Overlay:** Integration of forward-looking macroeconomic metrics (such as unemployment, GDP growth, HIBOR curves) to adjust Point-in-Time (PIT) defaults to Through-the-Cycle (TTC) PDs.

## 3. Why Outputs Are Not Formal Credit Approval

The system does **not** make underwriting decisions or loan approvals.
- **No Underwriting:** There is no automated credit decisioning engine. All proposed loan structures are labeled as `candidate_structure`.
- **Relationship Manager Review Required:** All recommendations are planning indicators that require manual relationship manager review, formal due diligence, borrower profiling, and compliance screening (KYC/AML).
- **Subject to Policy:** Terms, interest rates, and loan structures depend on bank credit policy, updated documentation, verified CDI data room records, and final collateral valuation.

## 4. Stress Test Assumptions

Stress scenarios apply deterministic shocks to examine the sensitivity of the company's financial posture:
1. **Rate Shock:** Floating-rate debt service is stressed by a custom HIBOR basis point shift. The model assumes all debt is variable-rate.
2. **Receivables Delay:** Average collection time is extended by custom DSO days. The model assumes a direct linear reduction in first-year operating cash flows.
3. **Input Cost Squeeze:** Material procurement cost (COGS) is stressed by a custom percentage. The model assumes zero price elasticity (the company cannot pass cost increases to customers).
4. **FX Stress:** USD-denominated COGS costs are shocked. The model assumes no currency hedging is in place other than the alternative collateral mitigation.

---

## 5. Facility Structuring & Sizing Constraints

Candidate facility structuring operates under strict credit policy rules:
- **DSCR Floor:** A strict DSCR floor of **1.10x** is required. Any scenario where stressed DSCR falls below this threshold triggers a watch/constraint alert.
- **LTV Cap:** Loan-to-Value (LTV) for secured/collateralized assets is capped at **70.0%** (0.70).
- **Maximum Individual Size:** Absolute limits are capped based on the company's historical revenue, working capital gap, and credit planning tier to protect cash-flow headroom.
- **Indicative Pricing:** Pricing is estimated as a spread above the reference HIBOR rate, reflecting the workspace planning tier.

## 6. Model Metadata & Governance

Every response generated by the advisory models includes standard metadata fields to ensure auditability:
- `model_version`: The specific code version of the calculation engine.
- `model_type`: E.g., `deterministic_scorecard`, `indicative_pd_proxy`, `scenario_stress_test`, `candidate_facility_structuring`, or `deterministic_valuation`.
- `calibration_status`: Explicitly identifies the calibration state (`uncalibrated_proxy`, `rules_based`, or `workspace_derived`).
- `assumptions`: List of specific baseline assumptions made during calculation.
- `limitations`: Explicit list of what the output does *not* represent.
- `data_quality`: Dictionary containing completeness indicators of inputs.
- `confidence_band`: Assessment (`low`, `medium`, `high`, `insufficient_data`) of the output reliability.
